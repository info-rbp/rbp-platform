Create a redesigned UX/UI structure for the Remote Business Partner website.

The goal is to reorganise the current website into a clearer, more commercial, easier-to-navigate platform experience. The website should present Remote Business Partner as an integrated business support ecosystem for small businesses, startups, and SMEs.

The design should feel modern, professional, practical, and business-focused. It should avoid looking like a generic consulting website. It should feel like a platform that combines advisory support, managed services, business applications, marketplace products, memberships, resources, finance/insurance pathways, offers, and support.

The website should be structured around the following top-level sections:

1. Home
2. On-Demand Services
3. Managed Services
4. Business Applications
5. Business Marketplace
6. Membership Hub
7. Resource Center
8. Operations Center
9. Business Offers
10. Help Center
11. Contact

Design the UX/UI around this new information architecture.

CURRENT PROBLEM TO SOLVE

The existing site has too many separate sections presented as individual navigation items. This creates a scattered experience. The redesign should consolidate related areas under clear parent sections and make the platform easier to understand.

The new design should make it clear that Remote Business Partner offers:
- flexible on-demand business services
- ongoing managed services
- white-labelled Frappe-powered business applications
- a business marketplace
- membership plans
- educational resources
- finance, insurance, and operational support pathways
- business offers and deals
- help and support

PRIMARY DESIGN OBJECTIVES

1. Simplify navigation.
2. Group related services and products under clear parent sections.
3. Make the platform easier to understand within the first few seconds.
4. Create strong conversion pathways for enquiries, memberships, marketplace purchases, and application setup requests.
5. Show that Remote Business Partner is more than a consulting website.
6. Position the platform as a business operating, advisory, and support ecosystem.
7. Make each section feel connected but distinct.
8. Support future growth without making the navigation feel overloaded.

UX FLOW REQUIREMENTS

A new visitor should be able to:
1. Understand what Remote Business Partner does from the Home page.
2. Choose between services, applications, marketplace, membership, resources, operations, offers, or help.
3. Browse the relevant section.
4. Understand the offering clearly.
5. Take action through sign-up, enquiry, purchase, quote/referral, or resource download.

A business owner looking for immediate help should be routed to:
On-Demand Services → Business Advisor / Services / DocuShare / Decision Desk.

A business owner looking for ongoing help should be routed to:
Managed Services → Bid Management / Real Estate / HR Services.

A business owner looking for software should be routed to:
Business Applications → Frappe Applications.

A business owner looking for packaged products should be routed to:
Business Marketplace.

A user looking for finance, insurance, or telecom/connectivity should be routed to:
Operations Center.

A user looking for education should be routed to:
Resource Center.

A user looking for discounts or partner offers should be routed to:
Business Offers.
